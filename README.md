# HGMMA
    ####Understanding the associations between microorganisms and metabolites is crucial for deciphering microbial functions, host metabolism, and disease mechanisms. However, experimentally identifying these associations is costly and time-consuming, and computational research specifically targeting the prediction of microbe-metabolite associations remains in its infancy. Furthermore, existing graph-based prediction methods often suffer from the underutilization of heterogeneous information, and treating unobserved associations as negative samples leads to false negatives. To address these challenges, we first integrated multiple public resources to construct a large-scale microbe-metabolite association dataset comprising 1,309 microorganisms, 1,596 metabolites, and 568,912 known associations. We then proposed HGMMA, a multi-view self-expression-enhanced heterogeneous graph contrastive learning model. HGMMA integrates multi-source similarity information with Graph Transformer-based representation learning to capture high-order structural features. Additionally, the model incorporates a connection strength-guided graph augmentation strategy and a multi-view self-expression mechanism designed to enhance homophily and mitigate the false negative issue inherent in heterogeneous graph contrastive learning. Experimental results demonstrate that HGMMA outperforms existing mainstream baseline methods, achieving an AUC of 0.9780 and an AUPR of 0.9770 in five-fold cross-validation. Ablation studies confirmed the effectiveness of the model's individual components. Case studies and external validation using the GMMAD dataset further substantiated the biological relevance, robustness, and generalization capability of the predicted associations. These results indicate that HGMMA provides an effective framework for discovering potential microbe-metabolite associations and offers strong support for research into microbiome-related mechanisms, biomarker discovery, and precision medicine.
